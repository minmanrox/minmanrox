My Story

I grew up in Northern Virginia, where I witnessed and admired the hard work and advanced technology that goes into national security. This ignited a passion for security in me and set me on the path toward a career in service of my nation.

At Texas A&M, I have received a top-notch education and gained valuable research experience. My coursework in computer engineering gives me a wide breadth of knowledge of computer systems, from the top-level software down to the binary hardware. My cybersecurity minor has given me skills in the fundamentals of cybersecurity. Working in the Secure and Trustworthy Hardware Lab, I have gained valuable research experience while also learning the principles of security at the hardware level. My commitment to academic success has allowed me to maintain a 4.0 GPA while also continuing to participate in undergraduate research.

My experience in both academics and research has given me a diverse set of skills. Collaborative projects in both my coursework and research have developed my soft skills including teamwork, communication, and project management. My research experience has given me valuable problem-solving and critical-thinking skills. I have also developed many hard skills, including hardware simulation and hardware fuzzing, cybersecurity, Python and C/C++ programming, software development, FPGA programming, and more.

My professional aspirations are guided by a sense of patriotism and a desire to protect others. A strong education in computer security enables me to fight against bad actors in the cyber realm. My goal is to pursue a career where I can apply these skills to protect against the most advanced threats to our national and global security.
